# N1-Definitions — Credit Underwriting
*2025 · ontological type: Methodology/Practice · 6 sources consulted*

## Ontological Type
**Methodology/Practice** — credit underwriting is a structured process applied by financial institutions to assess borrower creditworthiness before granting credit. It is standardized by banking regulators and supervisory authorities.

## Authoritative Definitions

### EBA — GL/2020/06 Loan Origination and Monitoring (2020)
> "Credit is granted to borrowers who, to the institution's best knowledge at the time of granting the credit, will be able to fulfil the terms and conditions of the credit agreement."

Source: EBA Guidelines on loan origination and monitoring (EBA/GL/2020/06) · https://www.eba.europa.eu/regulation-and-policy/credit-risk/guidelines-on-loan-origination-and-monitoring

### OCC — Office of the Comptroller of the Currency
> "Underwriting refers to the terms and conditions under which [banks] extend or renew credit, such as financial and collateral requirements, repayment programs, maturities, pricing, and covenants."

Source: OCC Comptroller's Handbook — Loan Portfolio Management · https://www.occ.gov/publications-and-resources/publications/comptrollers-handbook/index-comptrollers-handbook.html

## Industry Definitions

### ACAMS — Practitioner (independent)
> "Credit underwriting is the process by which a lender evaluates the risk of lending money to a borrower, assessing the borrower's ability and willingness to repay."

Source: ACAMS Glossary · https://www.acams.org *(independent practitioner association)*

### Underwrite.ai — [vendor]
> "Credit underwriting is the process of assessing whether a borrower is likely to repay a loan."

Source: https://underwrite.ai ⚠️ *vendor — not an independent source*

## Terminological Distinctions

| Term | Definition | Difference from Credit Underwriting |
|------|------------|--------------------------------------|
| Credit Scoring | Statistical model producing a numerical risk score | Scoring is one input to underwriting, not the full process |
| Credit Assessment | Broader evaluation of creditworthiness | Underwriting is the formal decision process with defined terms; assessment may be informal |
| Due Diligence | Investigation of a borrower's financial position | Due diligence feeds underwriting but covers a wider scope (legal, operational) |
| Loan Origination | End-to-end process from application to disbursement | Underwriting is a phase within origination |
